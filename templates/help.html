Frequently Asked Questions

How do I upload a medical report?

To upload a report, click on the "Upload Report" section in the sidebar, then click the "Choose File" button to select an image or PDF file from your device. After selecting the file, click "Analyze Report" to process it.

For best results, ensure your images are clear and well-lit, with text that is easily readable. PDF files should be text-based rather than scanned images for optimal analysis.

What file formats are supported?

MedAnalyzer supports JPG, PNG, and PDF files. For optimal results, we recommend:

  • Image files should be in JPG or PNG format with minimum 300 DPI resolution
  • PDF files should be text-based (not scanned images) for better accuracy
  • File size should not exceed 25MB
  • Ensure all text is clearly visible and not obscured by stamps or signatures
How accurate is the analysis?

Our AI-powered analysis has been validated against thousands of medical reports and achieves over 95% accuracy in identifying and interpreting lab values. The system uses advanced machine learning algorithms trained on millions of anonymized medical records and the latest clinical guidelines.

However, it should be used as a decision support tool and not as a replacement for professional medical judgment. Always review the AI analysis in the context of the patient's complete medical history and clinical presentation.

Is my data secure and private?

Yes, we take data security and patient privacy extremely seriously. All data is encrypted in transit using TLS 1.3 and at rest using AES-256 encryption. We are fully compliant with HIPAA, GDPR, and other relevant regulations.

Your data is never shared with third parties without your explicit consent. We do not sell or rent patient data. Our systems undergo regular security audits and penetration testing to ensure the highest level of protection for sensitive medical information.

How does the AI make recommendations?

Our AI system is trained on millions of anonymized medical records and the latest clinical guidelines from reputable sources like the American Medical Association, CDC, and WHO. It analyzes your report in the context of established medical knowledge, considering:

  • Parameter correlations and interactions
  • Established diagnostic criteria
  • Evidence-based treatment protocols
  • Patient demographics and risk factors
  • Latest research and clinical trials

The recommendations are generated using a transparent decision-making process that can be reviewed and audited.

Can I export my analysis?

Yes, you can export your analysis in multiple formats. Click the "Export & Print" option in the sidebar to save your report as:

  • PDF: Professional-looking document with charts and analysis
  • DOCX: Editable Word document for further customization
  • CSV: Comma-separated values for importing into spreadsheets
  • JSON: Structured data format for integration with other systems

You can also print directly from your browser or share via secure link with colleagues.

How do I interpret the health score?

The health score is a composite metric based on your lab results, with 100% representing optimal values across all tested parameters. The score is calculated using weighted importance of different biomarkers based on clinical significance:

  • 90-100%: Excellent health status
  • 80-89%: Good health status with minor areas for improvement
  • 70-79%: Fair health status, some abnormalities present
  • 60-69%: Poor health status, significant abnormalities
  • Below 60%: Critical health status, immediate attention needed

The score should be used as a general guide and interpreted in conjunction with the detailed analysis.

Can I compare multiple reports?

Yes, our Trend Analysis feature allows you to compare multiple reports over time. To use this feature:

  1. Go to the "Trend Analysis" section in the sidebar
  2. Select the patient from your patient list
  3. Choose the reports you want to compare (you can select multiple)
  4. Click "Generate Comparison" to create visualizations

The system will generate line charts showing changes in key biomarkers over time, highlight significant trends, and provide statistical analysis of changes. You can also set custom date ranges and compare against population averages.
